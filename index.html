Meet Uncle Alex

"A cigar is not just tobacco. It is patience made visible — the land, the rain, the years, and the hands of a man who truly cares."

Born in the cloud-laced highlands of Turrialba, Costa Rica, Alejandro — known to every cigar lover as Uncle Alex — grew up watching volcanic soil breathe life into the finest tobacco leaves on earth. Captivated by the legends of Havana, he left at twenty-two on a pilgrimage to Cuba's Vuelta Abajo valley — the most celebrated tobacco-growing region in the world.

For over a decade he apprenticed under the great master torcedor Don Rafael Ortega, learning the sacred geometry of the roll, the chemistry of fermentation, the alchemy of blending. He returned to Costa Rica carrying secrets no school can teach — and founded his small-batch estate on the volcanic slopes of Turrialba, where he personally selects every leaf and rolls every cigar to this day.

Costa Rica

The volcanic soil of Turrialba, enriched by centuries of eruption, produces tobacco leaves with an unmatched mineral richness and natural sweetness. Uncle Alex's estate sits at 1,400 meters — where the mist, the warmth, and the earth conspire to create something extraordinary. Every wrapper leaf on an Uncle Alex cigar begins here, on this sacred ground.

Cuba · Vuelta Abajo

The fillers and binders in Uncle Alex's blends draw from the legendary valleys of Vuelta Abajo — soil so perfect for tobacco that Columbus himself remarked upon it. Aged in Spanish cedar for a minimum of four years before rolling, these Cuban leaves bring depth, complexity, and that irreplaceable silky smoke that has seduced kings and presidents for five centuries.

How It's Made

The Ritual of
Every Cigar

01
The Harvest — Turrialba Highlands

Uncle Alex walks his rows at dawn, pinching leaves between his fingers, reading moisture and oil by touch alone. Only the top two primings make the cut — leaves that have absorbed years of volcanic mineral and tropical sun.

02
Pilón Fermentation — Cuban Method

Stacked in the traditional Cuban pilón method, leaves ferment for 18 months at carefully controlled temperatures. This process, learned in Vuelta Abajo, eliminates ammonia and transforms raw tobacco into the deep, complex leaf that defines the Uncle Alex character.

03
The Roll — Uncle Alex's Own Hands

Seated at his bench each morning, Alex rolls every cigar in the premium line personally. The chaveta, the bunching, the triple cap — movements so practiced they have become a form of meditation. No machine has ever touched an Uncle Alex cigar.

04
Cedar Aging — Six Months Minimum

Boxed in Spanish cedar and rested in the aging room where Andean winds keep the humidity perfect, each cigar sleeps until the blend has fully harmonized. The result: a smoke that speaks with one voice — complex, balanced, and unforgettable.
